Research Overview

Exploring Earth's internal structure through computational seismology

Research Focus

My research investigates the fundamental properties of Earth's interior using advanced seismological techniques and computational methods. I focus on understanding how seismic waves propagate through complex geological structures and what this reveals about our planet's deep processes.

Current Research Areas

Seismic Wave Propagation

Investigating how seismic waves travel through heterogeneous media, with particular focus on wave attenuation mechanisms and velocity structures in the Earth's mantle and core.

  • Full waveform modeling and inversion techniques
  • 3D wave propagation simulations
  • Attenuation tomography methods

Earth's Internal Structure

Using seismic observations to constrain models of Earth's interior, from the upper mantle to the inner core, to understand geodynamic processes and planetary evolution.

  • Core-mantle boundary investigations
  • Inner core anisotropy studies
  • Mantle transition zone properties

Computational Methods

Developing and applying advanced computational techniques for processing large seismic datasets and performing high-resolution numerical simulations.

  • Machine learning applications in seismology
  • High-performance computing for wave propagation
  • Data processing and analysis pipelines
